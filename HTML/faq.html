Getting Started

A Certificate of Eligibility (COE) is a determination letter from the VA. This letter is generated and sent to the student after they have applied for their VA education benefit; the VA determines if they are entitled to their desired VA education benefit. Once received, the student will provide the Veterans Resource Center with their COE to be reviewed and processed.

You can obtain a new Certificate of Eligibility (COE) based on your VA benefit chapter:

  • Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill®): View Statement of Benefits
  • Chapters 30 and 1606 (Montgomery GI Bill®): Access MGIB-SR Information
  • Chapter 35 (Dependents’ Educational Assistance): Apply for Education Benefits

Follow the instructions in the appropriate link to obtain or download your COE.

The Hazlewood Act is a State of Texas education benefit that provides qualified Veterans, spouses, and dependent children with up to 150 credit hours of tuition exemption at public institutions in Texas.

This exemption may cover a significant portion of tuition and certain fees; however, it does not cover:

  • Housing or meal plans
  • Books and supplies
  • Late fees or payment plan fees
  • Study abroad programs
  • Other living expenses

Students are responsible for any charges not covered by the exemption.

Tuition Assistance (TA) is a military benefit that helps eligible service members pay for tuition while on active duty.

Each branch of the military manages its own Tuition Assistance program, including eligibility requirements and application procedures.

To get started, contact your Educational Services Officer (ESO) or your branch’s Education Counselor for guidance on how to apply and use this benefit.
