Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

A Certificate of Eligibility (COE) is a determination letter from the VA. This letter is generated and sent to the student after they have applied for their VA education benefit; the VA determines if they are entitled to their desired VA education benefit. Once received, the student will provide the Veterans Resource Center with their COE to be reviewed and processed.

You can obtain a new Certificate of Eligibility (COE) based on your VA benefit chapter:

  • Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill): View Statement of Benefits
  • Chapters 30 and 1606 (Montgomery GI Bill): Access MGIB-SR Information
  • Chapter 35 (Dependents’ Educational Assistance): Apply for Education Benefits

Follow the instructions in the appropriate link to obtain or download your COE.

The Hazlewood Act is a State of Texas education benefit that provides qualified Veterans, spouses, and dependent children with up to 150 credit hours of tuition exemption at public institutions in Texas.

This exemption may cover a significant portion of tuition and certain fees; however, it does not cover:

  • Housing or meal plans
  • Books and supplies
  • Late fees or payment plan fees
  • Study abroad programs
  • Other living expenses

Students are responsible for any charges not covered by the exemption.

Tuition Assistance (TA) is a military benefit that helps eligible service members pay for tuition while on active duty.

Each branch of the military manages its own Tuition Assistance program, including eligibility requirements and application procedures.

To get started, contact your Educational Services Officer (ESO) or your branch’s Education Counselor for guidance on how to apply and use this benefit.

Applying & Processing

If you did not use your military educational benefits during a fall or spring term, you will typically need to resubmit a request for certification for the next term you plan to use your benefits.

VA benefits are not automatically renewed each semester. Students must request certification for every term they wish to receive benefits.

Be sure to submit all required documents and requests in a timely manner to avoid delays in processing.

Processing times can vary depending on the time of year and the volume of requests being received.

During peak enrollment periods (such as the beginning of fall and spring semesters), processing may take several weeks.

To help avoid delays, students are encouraged to:

  • Submit all required documents early
  • Ensure all forms are completed accurately
  • Monitor their student email for updates

If there is an issue with your request, the Veterans Benefits Processing Office will contact you using your official university email account.

Common issues may include:

  • Missing or incomplete documentation
  • Enrollment discrepancies
  • Eligibility concerns

It is important to check your email regularly and respond promptly to any requests for additional information to prevent delays in processing your benefits.

Payments & Financial Information

For certain benefits, such as Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill), the VA pays tuition and eligible fees directly to the school.

For other benefit programs, payments may be sent directly to the student instead.

It is important to understand how your specific benefit works and to ensure any remaining balance is paid by the university’s deadline.

Some VA education benefits, such as Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill), include a book and supplies stipend.

This payment is typically sent directly to the student at the beginning of the term.

The amount may vary depending on your enrollment status and the number of credit hours you are taking.

Yes, your Monthly Housing Allowance (MHA) is directly affected by your enrollment status.

Students enrolled full-time typically receive the full MHA rate, while those enrolled part-time receive a reduced amount.

If your enrollment falls below half-time, you may not be eligible for MHA.

Enrollment & Course Requirements

The VA only certifies courses that are required for your declared degree program.

If you are enrolled in classes that do not apply toward your degree, those courses will not be certified for VA benefits.

This may result in being certified for fewer credit hours than your total enrollment.

Full-time enrollment is typically 12 credit hours during a standard fall or spring semester for undergraduate students.

However, full-time status may vary depending on the length of the term and the type of program you are enrolled in.

Your benefit payments, including housing allowance, are based on your rate of pursuit (enrollment level).

If you are enrolled only in online courses, your Monthly Housing Allowance (MHA) may be reduced compared to students taking in-person classes.

The VA provides a different housing rate for students enrolled exclusively in distance learning.

Your tuition and fees may still be covered depending on your benefit, but housing payments will follow the online-only rate.

Hazlewood Act

The Hazlewood Exemption covers tuition and certain mandatory fees at public institutions in Texas.

Eligible students may receive up to 150 credit hours of exemption.

This benefit significantly reduces the cost of attending school but does not cover all expenses.

The Hazlewood Exemption does not cover the following:

  • Housing or meal plans
  • Books and supplies
  • Late registration or payment fees
  • Study abroad programs
  • Other personal or living expenses

Students are responsible for any costs not included in the exemption.

In most cases, Hazlewood cannot be used at the same time as certain federal VA benefits, such as Chapter 33 (Post-9/11 GI Bill), for the same term.

However, students may choose to use Hazlewood and other benefits in different semesters, depending on eligibility.

It is recommended to consult with the Veterans Benefits Processing Office to determine the best combination of benefits for your situation.

Academic Policies

Yes, you may drop a course while using VA educational benefits; however, it may affect your benefits.

Dropping a course can change your enrollment status, which may result in a reduction of benefits or a potential overpayment from the VA.

Before dropping a course, it is strongly recommended that you contact the Veterans Benefits Processing Office to understand how it may impact your benefits.

Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) is a standard used by the university to measure a student’s academic performance.

SAP typically includes requirements such as maintaining a minimum GPA, successfully completing a certain percentage of attempted courses, and making progress toward your degree.

Students must meet SAP requirements to remain eligible for financial aid and continue receiving VA educational benefits.

Academic probation or suspension may impact your ability to continue receiving VA educational benefits.

If you are placed on probation, you may still receive benefits for a limited time while working to improve your academic standing.

If you are suspended, your benefits may be terminated until you are reinstated and meet eligibility requirements again.

It is important to stay in good academic standing and communicate with both your academic advisor and the Veterans Benefits Processing Office.

Special Situations

If you take a break from enrollment during a Fall or Spring term, you may need to reapply or resubmit documentation before your benefits can be reactivated.

Your VA benefits are not automatically continued after a break in enrollment, so it is important to contact the Veterans Benefits Processing Office before returning to school.

Summer enrollment is evaluated separately from Fall and Spring terms, and benefit eligibility may vary based on your enrollment status and credit hours.

Full-time status, housing allowance, and payment amounts may differ depending on the length of the summer session (Mini, Summer I, Summer II, or 10-week term).

Students should review their enrollment carefully each summer term and submit a certification request as required.

Contact & Support
